About

Duke Technology Scholars (DTech) is a program aimed at young women who aspire to develop as engineers, computer scientists, and people in Silicon Valley. The pilot program began with its class of 2016, consisting of 12 young women ranging from first years to juniors, who interned in Silicon Valley and lived together in a house in Mountain View. The internships were at companies ranging from Fanatics, Apple, Carbon3D, LinkedIn, Intuit, Lifelock and more. The program also provides mentors, a move to extend the impact of the program beyond simply a summer internship.

DTech was founded by four incredible women, Vickie Gibbs, Sue Harnett, Monica Jenkins, Kimberly Jenkins. Their experiences have ranged from creating start up companies to working as engineers to working directly with people such as Bill Gates and Steve Jobs.
